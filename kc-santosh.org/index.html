Biography

Prof. KC (Casey) Santosh is an accomplished AI expert, TEDx Speaker, and Chair of Computer Science at the University of South Dakota (USD). He is the founding director of the AI Lab, where he leads cutting-edge research and education in artificial intelligence. With more than $8.7 million in funding from agencies like the DOD, NSF, and NIH, his work has produced 10 books and over 250 research papers in leading journals, including IEEE TPAMI and IEEE TMI.

Prof. Santosh's journey reflects strong international and industry collaborations. He served as a research fellow at the National Institutes of Health (NIH) and worked as a postdoctoral research scientist at the LORIA research centre, UniversitÃ© de Lorraine, in partnership with ITESOFT, France. He earned his PhD in Artificial Intelligence at the INRIA Nancy Grand Est research centre (France), where he developed expertise that continues to shape his research today.

At USD, he has transformed the AI program, achieving more than 3,000% growth in graduate enrollment within four years. He has also built interdisciplinary collaborations across biology, physics, biomedical engineering, sustainability, and business analytics, positioning USD as a leader in AI education. His achievements have been recognized with awards such as the Visionary Leadership Award (2023) and the Cutler Award for Teaching and Research Excellence (2021). Most recently, in 2024, he joined the NIST's AI Safety Institute Consortium and the U.S. Speaker Program, representing South Dakota on the national stage.
